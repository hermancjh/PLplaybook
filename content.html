Strategic Planning & the Total Learning Plan

Quick Start

Key takeaway: professional learning only works when it's traced through one unbroken chain — VMV → Creative Tensions → Strategic Thrusts → PL Objectives → ToLP. Skip a link and PL becomes optional.

Why it matters: this is the artefact chain every other Part of the playbook assumes you've already built.

Understand

Core concepts

  • Creative Tension — the productive gap between where the school aspires to be and where it currently is.
  • The 5-Stage Strategic Planning Process — how a school works through each stage of the cascade.
  • What the ToLP is / isn't — a co-developed strategic document, not a training calendar.

Research and rationale

Senge's concept of creative tension (1990) underlies the framing in this section — used to position the gap between vision and current reality as a source of energy, not a deficit.

Apply It

Reflection questions

Does our current strategic thrust statement name specific Learning Areas, Areas of Practice, and Practice Levels — or is it still at "grow our teachers" level of abstraction?

Discussion prompts

Use these three questions with your School Management Team to prioritise between competing tensions: Which is most central to this cycle's improvement goals? Which has the greatest impact on student learning if left unaddressed? Which is most actionable through professional learning right now?

Lead Others

Facilitation guide

Run a creative-tension-identification workshop with your SMT, modelled on the worked example's five steps: align on indicators, gather staff voice, review practice/outcome data together, articulate tensions as shared problems, and prioritise collectively.

Team discussion protocol

Use the three prioritisation questions from Level 4 verbatim as your SMT discussion structure — they're designed to be used as-is, not adapted.

School implementation considerations

At an Emerging-stage school, expect tension statements to surface culture issues as often as skill gaps — don't be surprised if the "PL problem" turns out to be a trust problem first. Check how your school compares →
